Project Recap

When Boulder Highway was constructed in 1931, the area was largely rural. Today, it is home to tens of thousands of houses, commerical lots, transit riders, daily vehicles. This growth increase has caused in increase in not only traffic for vehicles, bicycles, and pedestrians, but also in increase in pedestrian deaths.

With your help, we have re-imagined Boulder Highway to both accommodate growth, while improving safety, and setting a scalable foundation for future enhancements. From Charleston Boulevard to Wagon Wheel Drive, learn about the Reimagined Boulder Highway.

Early Action Items

0 - 2 Years | Estimated Cost: $16M

The first phase of implementation includes those items that can be installed relatively quickly and at low cost, but create a large safety and mobility benefit for the corridor.

Phase 1: Complete Streets

2 - 5 Years | Estimated Cost: $85-90M

In the Complete Streets phase, sidewalks are widened, jurisdictions determine whether the pedestrian area will include linear parks or a low-speed access lane, and improved lighting in updated areas is added.

Phase 2: Ultimate

Option 1 | 5+ Years | Estimated Cost: $40M

This is the final phase of the concept, bringing the vision to completion.
